# Turning Insights into Decisions

> **Learning Goal:** Learn how to communicate your data findings in a way that leads to real-world business actions.

---

##  The Value Chain

Analysts are not paid to produce "Data." They are paid to produce **Decisions**.

- **Data**: "Sales in Store C were 10 Lakhs last month." (A fact)
- **Insight**: "Store C had 20% lower footfall but 15% higher Average Order Value (AOV) than average." (A discovery)
- **Decision**: "Launch a loyalty program in Store C to increase visit frequency of high-value shoppers." (An action)

---

##  The 3-Act Structure for Data Stories

Just like a movie, a data presentation should have a beginning, middle, and end.

1. **The Setup (Act 1)**: What is the current state? (e.g., "Our sales are growing at 5% month-on-month").
2. **The Conflict (Act 2)**: What is the problem or opportunity? (e.g., "However, our return rates in 'Electronics' have doubled in Bangalore").
3. **The Resolution (Act 3)**: What should we do? (e.g., "We should switch the delivery partner for Bangalore to improve handling").

---

##  The Minto Pyramid (Executive Style)

**Why**: Executives are busy. They want the answer first, then the logic.

1. **Answer/Recommendation**: "We should close Store E."
2. **Main Arguments**: "Store E is losing 50k monthly and crime in that area has increased."
3. **Supporting Data**: "Profit/Loss chart and security logs."

**Where to use**: Use this in emails to managers or 5-minute boardroom updates.

---

##  Practice: Reframing your finding

**Weak Finding**: "The bar chart shows that UPI is used by 60% of people."
**Strong Insight**: "UPI is our primary growth driver. We should partner with PhonePe/GPay for an exclusive cashback offer to further reduce cash-handling costs."

---

##  Quick Check Questions

1. How is an "Insight" different from a "Data point"?
2. Why should you present the conclusion first to a CEO but maybe last to a fellow data scientist?
3. Turn this fact into an insight: "Refunds for 'Clothing' increased from 2% to 8% in July."
