# Building Interactive Reports

> **Learning Goal:** Learn how to turn a collection of charts into a cohesive, interactive system.

---

## 1. Cross-Filtering: Everything is Connected

**What**: In a good BI tool, clicking a bar in one chart automatically filters all other charts on the page.
**Example**: If you click 'Store A' in a bar chart, your line chart will automatically change to show only 'Store A' trends.
**Why**: This allows for "Deep Dive" discovery.

---

## 2. Tooltips: Data on Hover

**What**: Small pop-up boxes that appear when you hover your mouse over a data point.
**How**: You can customize these to show extra data (e.g., Mean, Std Dev, or even another small chart).
**Why**: Keeps the main dashboard clean while still providing detail to curious users.

---

## 3. Layout and Hierarchy (Where)

A professional dashboard follows a specific layout:
1. **Top**: KPI Cards (Important summary).
2. **Middle**: Main Trends (Line/Bar charts).
3. **Bottom**: Details (Table or Drill-down data).

---

##  Dashboard Design Checklist

Before you say "I'm finished," check your work:
- [ ] Do my colors have a meaning (e.g., Red = Low Profit)?
- [ ] Are my charts aligned properly (no messy spacing)?
- [ ] Does every chart have a clear, insight-driven title?
- [ ] Did I test my slicers to make sure the data actually changes?
